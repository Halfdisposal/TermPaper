The bosonic mode picture resolves the apparent contradiction by recognising that the *dynamical degree of freedom* of each EM mode — the oscillating amplitude — behaves exactly like a quantum harmonic oscillator, while the *travelling* nature of the wave is encoded in the spatial mode functions and the phase evolution of the coherent amplitude. When we say "bosonic mode," we mean a quantum degree of freedom obeying $[a^,a^†]=1[\hat{a}, \hat{a}^\dagger] = 1$
$[a^,a^†]=1$, whose excitations (photons) are bosons. The entire machinery of continuous variable quantum information — Gaussian states, symplectic transformations, the Wigner function, covariance matrices — is built on this identification, and it is what makes the analysis of quantum illumination and bosonic channel capacity tractable.
